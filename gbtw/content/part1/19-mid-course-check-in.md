**Mid-Course Check-In**

*Week 6 Reflection --- Halfway Point Review*

Part 1 --- Doc 19

You are halfway through the course. This is the moment to stop, look
back honestly at what you've built, and set clear intentions for the
second half. This document is a conversation with yourself --- not a
test.

**What the first six weeks were for**

Phase 1 rebuilt the habit and established core craft. Phase 2 went
deeper into character, emotion, and dialogue. By now you should have a
writing habit (however imperfect), a world taking shape, at least one
character who feels real, and a growing sense of what your prose does
well and where it struggles.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **If you've fallen behind:**

  Pick up from today and keep moving. The missed prompts will still be
  there. Momentum matters more than perfect completion.
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Six Reflection Questions**

Write your answers below. Honestly --- this is for you only.

**1. What are you writing better than six weeks ago?**

Compare your Day 1 free-write to something from Week 5 or 6. Name the
specific improvement.

**2. What is still consistently weak?**

Where do you still tell emotion instead of showing it? Where does
dialogue flatten? Where do you over-explain? Name it specifically.

**3. What does your writing keep returning to?**

Look across everything you've written. What themes, characters, images
recur even when unintended? This is the raw material of your voice.

**4. Is the habit holding?**

How many days did you actually write? If the habit is fragile --- what
is the obstacle, and what one change would make it more sustainable?

**5. What has the reading taught you?**

Which book or story produced the strongest effect? What specifically did
you learn as a writer --- what technique do you want to steal?

**6. What is the story you want to write?**

Not a full plot --- a feeling. A character, a world, a moral question,
an image. Describe it as clearly as you can. This is what the second
half is building toward.

**7. What are you building that could go further?**

Look at the world you're developing, the character taking shape, the
scenes you've written. Is any of it bigger than a short story? Not
better --- bigger. A short story can be exactly right for a contained
idea. But some material keeps generating questions the story hasn't
answered yet: history the protagonist doesn't know, a system whose costs
haven't been paid, a relationship that hasn't resolved.

You don't need to decide anything now. Part 2 exists precisely for this.
But if something you're working on feels like it has more rooms in it
than you've opened, note it here. That instinct is worth tracking.

**Intentions for the second half**

Write three intentions for Weeks 7--12. Not goals --- directions.
Examples: 'I intend to reach the end of a draft before revising.' 'I
intend to read more slowly and take notes.' 'I intend to write one
full scene per week.'

**What Phase 3 and 4 will bring**

The second half moves into story mechanics, constraint exercises, voice,
and the extra credit modules. The reading deepens --- Best Served Cold,
The Bloody Chamber, Crow, the screenplays, the craft essays. Campbell
moves toward completion.

The expectation by Day 84: one completed, revised short story. If you
haven't started one yet, start this week. Use doc 18. Use the character
and world you've been building. Write the turn.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **The halfway truth:**

  Most people who start something creative don't reach halfway. You did.
  Keep going.
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

*You're not behind. You're exactly where you are --- halfway through
something real.*
